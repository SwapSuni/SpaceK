V e n u s

Surface And Atmosphere

  • Venus is shrouded by an opaque layer of highly reflective clouds of sulfuric acid, preventing its surface from being seen from Earth in light.
  • Venus has the hottest surface of any planet in the Solar System, with a mean temperature of 737 K.
  • The mass of its atmosphere is 92 times that of Earth's, whereas the pressure at its surface is about 93 times that at Earth's.
  • Because of its runaway greenhouse effect, Venus has been identified by scientists such as Carl Sagan as a warning and research object linked to climate change on Earth.
  • Above the dense CO2 layer are thick clouds, consisting mainly of sulfuric acid.
  • Although Venus is closer than Earth to the Sun, it receives less sunlight on the ground.
  • The clouds reflect and scatter about 90% of the sunlight that falls on them back into space.

Characteristics

  • The highest point on Venus, Maxwell Montes, is therefore the coolest point on Venus, with a temperature of about 655 K.
  • Venus orbits the Sun at an average distance of about 0.72 AU, and completes an orbit every 224.7 days.
  • Venus rotates clockwise in retrograde rotation once every 243 Earth daysâ€”the slowest rotation of any planet. Because its rotation is so slow, Venus is very close to spherical.
  • To the naked eye, Venus appears as a white point of light brighter than any other planet or star (apart from the Sun).
  • Winds on Venus move at up to 60 times the speed of its rotation, whereas Earth's fastest winds are only 10-20% rotation speed.
  • Venus's extreme temperature and atmospheric pressure make water-based life as currently known unlikely.

Neighbours

  • Venus has no natural satellites.
  • It has several trojan asteroids: the quasi-satellite 2002 VE68 and two other temporary trojans, 2001 CK32 and 2012 XE133.
  • Alex Alemi's and David Stevenson's 2006 study shows Venus likely had at least one moon created by a huge impact event billions of years ago.
  • About 10 million years later, according to the study, another impact reversed the planet's spin direction and caused the Venusian moon gradually to spiral inward until it collided with Venus.
  • An alternative explanation for the lack of satellites is the effect of strong solar tides, which can destabilize large satellites orbiting the inner terrestrial planets.
  • Venus is the closest planet to Earth.
  • Venus "overtakes" Earth every 584 days as it orbits the Sun, it changes from the "Evening Star",to the "Morning Star".
