Surface And Atmosphere

  • The total surface area of Earth is about 510 million km2 Of this, 70.8% is below sea level and covered by ocean water.
  • The elevation of the land surface varies from the low point of -418 m at the Dead Sea, to a maximum altitude of 8,848 m at the top of Mount Everest.
  • The pedosphere is the outermost layer of Earth's continental surface and is composed of soil and subject to soil formation processes.
  • Earth's biosphere has significantly altered its atmosphere. Oxygenic photosynthesis evolved 2.7 Gya, forming the primarily nitrogen-oxygen atmosphere of today.
  • Earth's atmosphere has no definite boundary, gradually becoming thinner and fading into outer space.

Characteristics

  • While large volumes of water can be found throughout the Solar System, only Earth sustains liquid surface water.
  • Water vapor is widely present in the atmosphere and forms clouds that cover most of the planet.
  • Greenhouse gases in the atmosphere like carbon dioxide trap a part of the energy from the Sun close to the surface.
  • Earth rotates around its own axis in slightly less than a day (in about 23 hours and 56 minutes). Earth's axis of rotation is tilted with respect to the perpendicular to its orbital plane around the Sun, producing seasons.
  • During the first billion years of Earth's history, the ocean formed and then life developed within it.
  • Humans emerged 300,000 years ago, and have reached a population of almost 8 billion today.
  • A planet that can sustain life is termed habitable, even if life did not originate there.

Neighbours

  • Earth is orbited by one permanent natural satellite, the Moon.
  • It orbits Earth at 380,000 km (1.3 light seconds) and is roughly a quarter as wide as Earth.
  • The Moon always faces the Earth with the same side through tidal locking and causes tides, stabilizes Earth's axis, and gradually slows its rotation.
  • The most widely accepted theory of the Moon's origin, the giant-impact hypothesis, states that it formed from the collision of a Mars-size protoplanet called Theia with the early Earth.
  • The Moon may have dramatically affected the development of life by moderating the planet's climate.
  • Earth's co-orbital asteroids population consists of quasi-satellites, objects with a horseshoe orbit and trojans.
  • There are approx 5000 operational, human-made satellites orbiting Earth.
